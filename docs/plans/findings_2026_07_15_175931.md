## Design decisions

- Multi-pool is a manual-booking capability only; scan and exact booking remain single-account.
- The Web API adds `account_mode=multi_pool` and exactly two `user_keys`, while retaining legacy `user_key`.
- Secrets are passed once through child-process stdin, not argv or account-identifying logs.
- The coordinator combines per-account pacing with a global adjacent-hour lease ledger.
- A timeout permanently tombstones that logical hour for the run, including after stable absence.
- The second account starts 0.35 seconds after the first account's adjacent-hour assignment; later writes obey both account and global backoff.

## Independent implementation review

- The first implementation review found two release blockers: concurrent HTTP starts could create isolated coordinators, and the deployment mode was enforced only by the Web process.
- The server now atomically makes a multi-pool job exclusive against other manual booking jobs, while preserving legacy concurrent single-account jobs when no pool is active.
- The engine now rejects `off`, forces `dry_run`, and permits real final submission only in explicit `live` mode.
- Review also identified missing structured lease/cooldown events, a serial-only lease test, and no multi-pool `guided-fast` integration test. All three gaps are implemented and covered locally.
- The first re-review closed the P0/P1 findings and found one residual P2 invariant: a coordinator account slot could acquire both in-flight hours even though the main scheduler did not assign it that way.
- The coordinator now rejects `account_slot_in_flight`; the thread-barrier test proves same-hour single ownership and distinct `pool_1/pool_2` ownership across the adjacent in-flight pair.
- The final independent read-only re-review found no remaining P0-P2 issue. Publication may proceed.
